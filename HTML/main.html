Teimour Radjabov
broke onto the world chess stage as a teenager, earning
the grandmaster title just after his 14th birthday and
scoring a stunning win against Garry Kasparov as
a 15-year-old in Linares 2003, the first time in seven years
Kasparov had lost a rated game with the white pieces.
That game was also symbolic, as Radjabov was born in
the same city as the “Beast from Baku” and grew up playin
the same hyper-aggressive openings that Kasparov
had championed in his youth.

Fabiano Caruana
The prodigy who broke the record held by Hikaru
Nakamura for America's youngest chess grandmaster,
Fabiano Caruana has climbed the mountain of chess and
reached the summit. This young great master has had a
ranking as high as number-two in the world and has won
numerous tournaments in his career. In 2018 the American
faced Magnus Carlsen at the World Chess Championship
in London in 2018, losing in the quick playoffs.
